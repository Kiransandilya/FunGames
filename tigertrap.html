TIGER TRAP

Bagh-Chal · you herd the goats

To drop: 20 On board: 0 Eaten: 0/5
Place a goat on any point.

HOW TO PLAY

You are the goats. Four tigers (orange) start in the corners and move every turn. You drop 20 goats, one per turn, onto any empty point.

A tiger captures by leaping over a goat on a line into the empty point beyond — just like checkers. If the tigers eat 5 goats, you lose.

Once all 20 goats are down, you slide them one point along the lines. You win by surrounding the tigers so not one of them can move.

Goats in danger of being eaten flash red. Wall the tigers in — your only weapon is your numbers.

Bagh-Chal ("moving tigers") is a centuries-old game of the Nepali hills, scratched into stone or wood and played with pebbles and seeds — the herder's daily fear of the tiger made into a game of nerve.
